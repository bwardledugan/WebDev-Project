Languages I've Learned

These are the 5 languages I've learned a considerable amount of throughout my life.

  • English
    • English is my first language. I grew up in the United States, so I learned English throuhgout my life.

  • French
    • French is my second language. I studied French all throughout high school and in my first year of college, making 5 years in total. I learned French so that I could communicate with my family that live in French Polynesia.

  • Portuguese
    • Portuguese is my third language. Though technically my third language, I am far more proficient in my ability to speak Portuguese than any other romance language. I served as a missionary for the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints for 8 months in Recife, Brazil. I had to learn the language quickly as to communicate with people and survive. I loved living there, and my speaking abilities have actually improved after my return to the United States due to a wider variety of study materials.

  • Spanish
    • Spanish is my fourth language. I learned Spanish quickly after returning from my short mission in Brazil. The language's similarity to Portuguese made it the easiest language for me to learn. While serving as a missionary in New Mexico, I had to learn Spanish quickly in order to communicate with the Hispanic people living in a city called Grants.

  • Navajo
    • Navajo is my fifth and least proficient language. I learned a bit of the language while serving as a missionary in the Navajo Nation. I learned just enough to introduce myself, thank someone, and make the old people laugh.
